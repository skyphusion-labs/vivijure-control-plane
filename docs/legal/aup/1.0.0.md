You are signing up for a studio that Skyphusion Labs runs for you. That makes this different from

## 1. The absolute red line: child sexual abuse material (CSAM)

**This is not a rule we balance against other rules. It is the line the whole project is built
around, and it is absolute.**

You may not use the hosted studio to create, generate, attempt to generate, request, solicit, train
a model for, store, or distribute any child sexual abuse material: any sexual or sexualized
depiction of a minor, or of any person who is or appears to be a minor.

**There is no exception, and specifically:**

- It applies to **synthetic, AI-generated, "fake," fictional, cartoon, drawn, virtual, and
  "pretend" depictions** exactly as much as to a photograph of a real child. It does not matter
  that no real minor was photographed, involved, or harmed. It does not matter that the output is
  "just AI," a drawing, or a fictional character. Pretend does not make it acceptable. There is no
  artistic, satirical, fictional, "it is not real," or "age-play" exception.
- It covers **every generation path in the studio**: text-to-image and image keyframes,
  image-to-video motion, model/LoRA training, and the finish modules, including audio-driven
  lip-sync and any face, voice, or likeness module. "Aging down" any real or generated person into
  sexualized content is covered, and prohibited.

**This is also the law, not only our policy.** United States federal law criminalizes
computer-generated and synthetic child sexual abuse material, and obscene visual depictions of
minors, even where no actual child was involved, including under **18 U.S.C. 1466A** (obscene
visual representations of the sexual abuse of children, reaching drawings, cartoons, and
computer-generated images) and **18 U.S.C. 2252A**. Many other countries prohibit the same conduct,
often more broadly. The absence of a real child is not a defense, and it is not one we recognize.

**What we do about it.** CSAM is the single exception to our otherwise hands-off posture (Section
5). On the hosted studio:

- **We refuse and remove.** Access is terminated immediately and permanently, without warning.
- **We preserve.** We preserve the relevant material and records as the law requires.
- **We report.** We report to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) through
  its CyberTipline, and cooperate with law enforcement. Because we operate this service, reporting
  is a **legal obligation** for us under 18 U.S.C. 2258A, not merely a promise. We would do it
  anyway.

Our privacy stance is not a shield for this, and a hosted studio is not a safe place to do it. The
full operational posture, including what we do and do not scan for, is in `../ABUSE-AND-NCMEC.md`.

## 2. Other prohibited content and uses

You may not use the hosted studio to create, train models for, or distribute:

### 2.1 Non-consensual intimate imagery (NCII)
Sexual or nude depictions of a real, identifiable person created or shared without that person's
consent. This includes "undressing" or sexualizing images of real people, and intimate content
generated to look like a specific real person without their consent.

### 2.2 Non-consensual deepfakes and likeness / publicity-rights abuse
Realistic depictions of a real, identifiable person without their consent, especially where the
result is intended or likely to deceive, defame, defraud, harass, or exploit. This includes:
- Putting words or actions onto a real person they did not say or do, presented as real.
- Using a person's face, voice, or likeness (including via a trained model/LoRA built from images
  of them) without consent, including for commercial gain.
- Impersonating a real person or organization to deceive.

Consensual creative work involving a person who has actually agreed, and clearly-labeled satire or
commentary that does not deceive, are different. The burden is on you to have that consent and to
stay out of the harms above.

### 2.3 Hateful, harassing, and violent content
- Content that demeans, dehumanizes, or incites hatred or violence against people based on a
  protected characteristic.
- Targeted harassment, bullying, threats, or content created to intimidate or stalk a specific
  person.
- Content that promotes or instructs terrorism or mass violence.

### 2.4 Other illegal or harmful use
- Anything illegal under applicable law, or that facilitates an illegal act.
- Fraud, scams, phishing, or disinformation campaigns; forged documents, currency, or identity
  documents.
- Malware, or content designed to compromise systems.
- Infringing other people's copyright, trademark, or other intellectual-property rights.
- Attempts to break, evade, or abuse the platform: bypassing the access gate, evading rate limits
  or quotas, scraping, attacking the control plane, or trying to reach another tenant's studio,
  data, or storage.

### 2.5 Adult sexual content (consenting adults)
Adult sexual content involving consenting adults is **permitted on the hosted studio**, subject to
Sections 1, 2.1, and 2.2, which remain absolute regardless. You are responsible for the legality of
what you make where you are.

## 3. Your responsibilities

- You are responsible for what you generate, upload, train on, and download.
- You confirm you have the rights and any required consent for images, audio, and likenesses you
  feed into the studio.
- You will not use outputs in a way that breaks the law or this policy after they leave the studio.
- **You are an adult.** The hosted studio is not offered to children and you must be old enough to
  form a binding agreement where you live.
- It is your responsibility to comply with the laws that apply to you and the place you operate
  from.

### 3.1 Your RunPod account is yours, and RunPod's rules apply to it

The hosted studio renders on GPU endpoints that run on **your own RunPod account**, which you
create and fund. That means:

- **The relationship with RunPod is yours, not ours.** Your use of RunPod is governed by RunPod's
  own terms and acceptable-use policy, which bind you directly and independently of this policy.
  RunPod's content rules may prohibit things this policy allows.
- **Their bill is yours.** GPU time bills to your RunPod balance. We do not pay it and cannot
  refund it.
- **We cannot reach into your RunPod account.** We create the endpoints you authorize us to create
  and nothing else. If you want them gone, you delete them, and we show you how.

## 4. Enforcement: what we will actually do

We keep enforcement proportionate but firm, and we state our real levers rather than bluff:

- **CSAM:** immediate, permanent termination without warning, plus preservation and reporting to
  NCMEC and law enforcement (Section 1). No warning, no appeal.
- **Other serious violations** (NCII, non-consensual deepfakes used to harm, targeted harassment,
  clearly illegal use): suspension or termination, content removal, and reporting where the law
  requires or the harm warrants.
- **Lesser or ambiguous violations:** we may warn, restrict, suspend, or remove content at our
  discretion.

**The levers we actually hold**, so you know exactly what "enforcement" means here:

| Lever | What it does | Reach |
|---|---|---|
| **Suspend** | Routing to your studio is refused instantly at the control plane. Immediate, reversible. | Our infrastructure |
| **Terminate** | Your account and your tenant studio (Worker, database, storage) are deleted. | Our infrastructure |
| **Preserve** | We retain material and records where the law requires it (Section 1). | Our infrastructure |
| **Your RunPod endpoints** | **We cannot delete or disable them.** They are on your account. On termination we tell you what to delete. | Yours, not ours |

We may act on a good-faith belief that a violation occurred, and we are not obligated to host
content while we look into it.

## 5. What we can see (the honest version)

We will not pretend to a level of blindness we do not have. On the hosted studio:

- **Your studio data lives on our Cloudflare account.** Your database and your file storage are
  ours to administer, so **we are technically capable of reading your projects, prompts, cast
  images, and finished films.** That is a fact of the architecture, not a permission you granted us
  for content review.
- **We do not look, and we do not scan.** We run no proactive monitoring, no content scanning, and
  no automated review of what you generate. We do not use your content to train models, we do not
  profile you, and we do not sell anything about you. We access tenant data only where it is
  necessary to run or repair the service, where you ask us to, where the law compels us, or where
  we are acting on a report under Section 1 or 4.
- **The one exception is CSAM** (Section 1), and it overrides everything above.
- **If you want a studio we genuinely cannot see, self-host it.** Vivijure is AGPL software, the
  hosted control plane included, and the self-hosted studio never talks to us. That option is real,
  it is free, it has every feature this one has (see `../PARITY-COMMITMENT.md`), and we will point
  you to it rather than talk you out of it.

The full data picture, including who is a controller and who is a processor, is in the Privacy
Policy and `../PRIVACY-DELTA.md`.

## 6. Reporting abuse

Report content or use that violates this policy on the hosted studio to **abuse@skyphusion.org**.

For suspected CSAM specifically, report it directly to NCMEC (CyberTipline, `report.cybertip.org`)
and/or law enforcement, in addition to reporting to us. If you see it, report it.

When you report, include enough to locate the content (what, where, when) without yourself
downloading, copying, or redistributing illegal material.

Reports are handled in good faith, most serious first (CSAM and imminent-harm reports ahead of
everything), acting on what can be verified, and without retaliation against good-faith reporters.
